What is Health Psychology?

Going beyond traditional medicine to understand how your mind, behavior, and social environment influence your physical health.

The Bridge Between Mind and Body

Health Psychology involves keeping people healthy, preventing illness, and helping those who are ill manage their condition. While a medical doctor treats the physical symptoms of a disease, a Health Psychologist looks for the psychological roots and behavioral patterns that may be contributing to the illness or hindering recovery.

We address the "human factor" in medicine. For example, why do some people strictly follow medical advice while others don't? How does chronic stress physically damage the cardiovascular system over time? How can we use the power of the mind to manage chronic pain?

The Biopsychosocial Model: A Deeper Look

Most modern behavioral medicine relies on this framework, which posits that health is a result of three distinct but overlapping areas:

  • Biological Factors:
    This includes your genetics, neurochemistry, physical trauma, and the body's physiological response to stress. We can't change your DNA, but we can help you manage how your body expresses stress.
  • Psychological Factors:
    Your personality type, coping mechanisms, beliefs about health, and emotional state. For instance, optimism is scientifically proven to boost immune function.
  • Social Factors:
    Your support network, cultural background, socioeconomic status, and family dynamics. Isolation is as big a risk factor for mortality as smoking.

Health Psychology vs. Clinical Psychology

It is common to confuse the two. While both deal with mental health, their primary focus differs:

  • Clinical Psychology typically focuses on treating mental health disorders like schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, or severe depression that may be unrelated to physical health.
  • Health Psychology specifically focuses on how mental health issues (like anxiety or depression) are caused by or are causing physical illness. We treat the person to heal the body.

When Should You See a Health Psychologist?

You might benefit from our services if you are facing:

Chronic Diagnosis

Adjusting to life with diabetes, cancer, or heart disease.

Unexplained Symptoms

Physical pain or fatigue that doctors cannot find a clear medical cause for (psychosomatic).

Lifestyle Change

Need support quitting smoking, losing weight, or improving sleep hygiene.

Medical Phobias

Overcoming fear of needles, surgery, or hospitals.

Our Therapeutic Approach

We use evidence-based interventions such as Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) tailored for medical adherence, Biofeedback to teach you to control heart rate and tension, and Motivational Interviewing to empower you to make lasting life changes.
